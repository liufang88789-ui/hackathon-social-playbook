# Breakout Hackathon Project X Patterns

## What worked in past Colosseum hackathons

### Narrative angles that gained traction
1. "We were building this when..." — founder journey angle
2. "The problem we actually care about..." — authentic pain point
3. "We just shipped the thing everyone said was impossible..." — underdog/technical flex
4. "Why we're choosing Solana over [competing chain]..." — chain tribalism
5. Thread format with numbered insights performs 3x better than single tweets

### Content formats that worked best
- Daily devlog threads (ship something every day, even if small)
- Hot takes on ecosystem problems (provocative,会引起讨论)
- "What we learned from failing at X" — vulnerability resonates
- Screenshots of working product + "it works!" energy
- Spaces participation — especially AMAs with other builders
- Quote tweets on relevant topics from ecosystem leaders

### Timing patterns
- Post during US morning (14:00-18:00 UTC) for maximum engagement
- Hackathon final week = peak attention, post aggressively then
- Day 1: Project intro + why this matters
- Day 2-3: Progress updates + technical challenges solved
- Day 4-5: Working demo posts + call for feedback
- Day 6-7: Final countdown + what we've built thread

### What got buried
- One-off announcements without thread backup
- Overly polished launch posts (came across as fake)
- Too many memes before establishing credibility
- Ignoring comments/DMs in first 48 hours
- Posting at night (UTC) — low engagement
